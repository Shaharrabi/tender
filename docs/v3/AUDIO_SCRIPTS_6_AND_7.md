# Script 6: "This Is Tender"
## Homepage Onboarding Video Narration

**Purpose:** Short, inspiring video narration for homepage/onboarding — explains the app, process, and benefits  
**Format:** Voiceover for a 60-90 second video with visuals  
**Duration:** ~75 seconds  
**Voice Direction:** Confident but warm. Grounded. Like someone who has been through it and knows the way. NOT salesy, NOT clinical. Think: the opening of a documentary you can't stop watching.  
**Visual Notes:** Pair with slow, beautiful footage or animation — couples in real moments (not stock perfection), nature, hands touching, morning light, someone pausing before speaking. The visuals should breathe.

---

**[SCRIPT BEGINS]**

Every couple has a dance.

[pause - 2s]

A pattern you fall into when things get hard. One of you reaches. The other pulls away. And round and round you go — same fight, different day.

[pause - 2s]

It's not because something is wrong with you. It's because both of you learned, long before you met each other, how to protect yourselves. And those protections — they made sense once. But they're costing you now.

[pause - 2s]

Tender is built on one idea: you can't change what you can't see.

[pause - 1.5s]

So we start with seeing. Five research-backed assessments that map your attachment style, your personality, how you handle conflict, what you value, and where your nervous system goes under stress.

[pause - 1.5s]

Then we help you feel it. Not just understand it — actually make contact with what's underneath.

[pause - 1.5s]

Then we help you shift it. Guided practices, a personalized growth plan, and an AI coach named Sage who knows your patterns and meets you where you are.

[pause - 2s]

This isn't a quiz that tells you your "type" and leaves you there. This is a process. See yourself. Understand your partner. Learn new moves. Practice them. Together.

[pause - 2s]

The science of relationships says the same thing over and over: it's not about finding the right person. It's about becoming the right partner.

[pause - 2s]

That's what Tender is for.

[pause - 1.5s]

Start with yourself. Grow together.

**[SCRIPT ENDS]**

**Total word count:** ~210 words  
**At narration pace:** ~70-80 seconds

# Script 7: "Before We Begin"
## Self-Discovery Journey Intro

**Purpose:** The first audio a user hears when they begin the assessment/self-discovery process. Sets the emotional tone for everything that follows. Plays before the first assessment question appears.  
**Format:** Solo listening, headphones recommended  
**Duration:** ~4 minutes  
**Voice Direction:** This is the most intimate of all the scripts. Slow. Spacious. Like someone sitting across from you in a quiet room who genuinely cares how you're doing. No performance. No inspiration. Just presence.  
**When it plays:** After the user taps "Begin Your Journey" and before the first assessment loads

---

**[SCRIPT BEGINS]**

Before we begin — I want you to know something.

[pause - 3s]

What you're about to do takes courage. You're choosing to look at yourself honestly. Not everyone does that. Most people spend their whole lives on autopilot — reacting to their partner the way they always have, wondering why it keeps going the same way.

You're here because some part of you knows there's more.

[pause - 5s]

Over the next little while, you're going to answer some questions. They'll ask about how you feel in close relationships. How you handle conflict. What matters to you. How your emotions move through you.

Some of the questions will feel easy. Some might bring something up. That's okay. There are no right answers. There's only what's true for you.

[pause - 5s]

Here's what I want you to keep in mind as you go through this:

[pause - 2s]

First — this is not a test. Nobody passes or fails. Your results won't tell you you're broken. They'll show you your patterns — the ways you've learned to navigate love and closeness. Every pattern made sense at some point. We're just going to look at them together.

[pause - 5s]

Second — be honest. Not the version of yourself you wish you were. Not the version your partner wants you to be. The real version. The one who sometimes shuts down. Or pushes too hard. Or says "I'm fine" when you're not.

That version is welcome here. Actually — that version is necessary. Because that's where the real work begins.

[pause - 5s]

Third — this is just the beginning. The assessments will give you a map. But the map isn't the territory. After this, you'll have a portrait of how you move through relationships — your strengths, your edges, your patterns. And then, piece by piece, you'll have the chance to grow.

Not by becoming someone else. By becoming more of who you already are — with more awareness, more skill, and more choice.

[pause - 5s]

One more thing.

[pause - 2s]

If you're doing this with a partner — whether they're doing it alongside you or whether you're going first — remember: this part is yours. Your answers. Your patterns. Your story.

Later, if you both choose to, you can bring your stories together and see how they interact. That's where the relationship work lives. But right now, this is about you. Getting clear on what you bring to the table — not to judge it, but to understand it.

[pause - 5s]

So — take a breath.

[pause - 4s]

Put your phone somewhere comfortable. If you can, find a quiet spot. This doesn't need to be rushed.

[pause - 3s]

And when you're ready — we'll begin.

[pause - 5s]

You're already doing the hard part. You showed up.

Let's see what's here.

**[SCRIPT ENDS]**

**Total word count:** ~430 words  
**At narration pace:** ~4 minutes

## Recording Order (Recommended)

Record in this order for consistent energy and voice:

1. **Script 7: "Before We Begin"** — Record this first while your voice is fresh and calm. This is the most intimate script and needs the most settled energy.

2. **Script 6: "This Is Tender"** — Record second. This needs slightly more energy and confidence than Script 7, but you're still warmed up.

3. **Script 5: "Knowing Your Dance"** — The foundational practice. Longest script. Record while you still have stamina.

4. **Script 1: "Finding Each Other Again"** — Warm, tender energy.

5. **Script 4: "The Repair Conversation"** — Structured, grounding energy.

6. **Script 3: "What Matters Most"** — Slightly more energized, aspirational.

7. **Script 2: "The Space Between"** — This one benefits from a voice that sounds like it understands urgency. Good to record when you have a bit more activation in your own system.

**Total recording time estimate:** 2-3 hours (including breaks, retakes, and setup)
