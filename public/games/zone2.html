Practice · Window Awareness
Know Your
Window
The window of tolerance is where you can think clearly, feel your feelings, and stay connected to others.

Outside it, you are either too activated (fight/flight) or too shut down (freeze).
Signals
Above the Window
When you are above your window — hyperaroused — you might feel:

Racing heart. Racing thoughts. Anger. Anxiety. Urgency. The need to do something.
WHEN I AM ABOVE MY WINDOW, I NOTICE...
Signals
Below the Window
When you are below your window — hypoaroused — you might feel:

Foggy. Numb. Disconnected. Empty. The urge to withdraw or disappear.
WHEN I AM BELOW MY WINDOW, I NOTICE...
Return
Finding Center
The breath is your doorway back. Not just any breath — but conscious, slow breath that tells your nervous system: you are safe.
The 4-7-8 breath:

Inhale for 4 counts.
Hold for 7 counts.
Exhale for 8 counts.

The long exhale activates your rest system.
Integration
Carry This
When you feel yourself leaving your window — pause.

Ask: "Am I above or below?"

Then: one slow breath. That is the return.
