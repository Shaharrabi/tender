## PART 1: TOP 5 EVIDENCE-BASED RELATIONAL ASSESSMENTS

### 1. **Experiences in Close Relationships - Revised (ECR-R)**
**The Gold Standard for Adult Attachment**

- **What it measures**: Two dimensions—Attachment Anxiety (fear of abandonment, hypervigilance about partner availability) and Attachment Avoidance (discomfort with closeness, emotional distancing)
- **Format**: 36 items (short forms available: ECR-12, ECR-S)
- **Research strength**: Decades of validation across cultures; 85% shared variance stability over 3 weeks; explains 30-40% of between-person variation in relationship emotions
- **Why include**: Attachment style is the single most powerful predictor of relationship patterns, including the pursue-withdraw dynamic. Understanding each partner's attachment orientation is foundational to any intervention strategy
- **App application**: Baseline assessment, periodic re-assessment, predictor of which interventions will resonate with each partner

**Sources**: Fraley et al., Wei et al. (2007), Lafontaine et al. (2016)

---

### 2. **RELATE Assessment**
**The Most Comprehensive Relationship Evaluation**

- **What it measures**: 10 major aspects of relationship wellness—personality, contexts/backgrounds, individual traits, couple traits, relationship satisfaction, communication, conflict resolution, childhood experiences, attitude alignment, and problem areas
- **Format**: ~300 items; produces 24-page comparative report
- **Research strength**: 40+ years of validation; 100,000+ couples; data shows taking the assessment alone produces positive outcomes (therapeutic effect of reflection)
- **Why include**: Most thoroughly validated comprehensive assessment; excellent for initial "state of the relationship" baseline
- **App application**: Comprehensive onboarding assessment, identifies specific intervention targets, generates personalized report

**Sources**: Utah State University/RELATE Foundation research

---

### 3. **Gottman Relationship Checkup**
**Based on 50 Years of "Love Lab" Research**

- **What it measures**: Friendship & intimacy, conflict management, shared meaning, trust & commitment, plus specific domains (finances, parenting, in-laws, housework, sex, individual concerns)
- **Format**: 337 questions producing detailed report mapped to "Sound Relationship House"
- **Research strength**: Gottman's research predicts divorce with 94% accuracy; identifies specific behavioral patterns (Four Horsemen) that undermine relationships
- **Why include**: Most empirically-grounded understanding of what makes relationships succeed or fail; maps directly to proven intervention framework
- **App application**: Periodic "checkup" assessment; tracks progress on specific domains; flags emergence of destructive patterns

**Sources**: Gottman Institute research spanning 40+ years

---

### 4. **Couples Satisfaction Index (CSI)**
**Brief but Powerful Satisfaction Tracking**

- **What it measures**: Global relationship satisfaction as a unidimensional construct
- **Format**: Available in 32, 16, or 4-item versions
- **Research strength**: Exceptionally high correlation with longer measures; superior psychometric properties to legacy measures (DAS, RAS); sensitive to change over time
- **Why include**: Ideal for frequent progress tracking without assessment fatigue; research-validated "thermometer" for relationship health
- **App application**: Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins; tracks intervention effectiveness; early warning system for relationship decline

**Sources**: Funk & Rogge (2007)

---

### 5. **Personal Assessment of Intimacy in Relationships (PAIR)**
**Multidimensional Intimacy Mapping**

- **What it measures**: Five types of intimacy—Emotional, Social, Sexual, Intellectual, and Recreational intimacy
- **Format**: 36 items with good research support
- **Research strength**: Captures the multifaceted nature of closeness; helps identify specific intimacy deficits
- **Why include**: Intimacy is distinct from satisfaction; couples can be satisfied but lack certain intimacy dimensions; targeted intimacy-building interventions
- **App application**: Identifies which intimacy domains need attention; personalizes suggestions for connection activities

**Sources**: Schaefer & Olson research; Hope Clinical Research Labs

---

### 6. **Big Five Personality (IPIP-NEO or Similar)**
**Understanding How Your Hard-Wiring Interacts**

- **What it measures**: Five core personality dimensions—Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism (OCEAN)
- **Format**: Full IPIP-NEO is 300 items; shorter validated versions available (IPIP-NEO-120, BFI-44, BFI-10)
- **Research strength**: Neuroticism is the #1 personality predictor of relationship distress; Agreeableness predicts conflict style; Conscientiousness predicts follow-through on relationship commitments
- **Why include**: Attachment explains *what* triggers us relationally; personality explains *how intensely* we react and *what style* of reaction we default to. The interaction effects matter enormously—High Neuroticism + Low Agreeableness paired with Avoidant attachment = explosive potential
- **App application**: Helps partners understand each other's "operating system"; predicts where friction will arise; informs communication style recommendations

**Sources**: Costa & McCrae research; IPIP collaborative

## PART 2: FOUNDATIONAL THERAPEUTIC MODALITIES

### Tier 1: Strongest Evidence Base (Meta-Analyses, Large RCTs)

#### **EMOTIONALLY FOCUSED THERAPY (EFT)**
*Dr. Sue Johnson — "Hold Me Tight"*

**Core Theory**:
- Relationship distress = attachment injury
- Negative cycles (pursue-withdraw) are attempts to meet attachment needs
- Healing comes through accessing and expressing "softer" vulnerable emotions
- Creating secure attachment bond through new emotional experiences

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **A.R.E.**: Accessible, Responsive, Engaged (markers of secure bond)
- **Negative Cycles**: Identify and de-escalate pursue-withdraw, attack-defend patterns
- **Primary vs. Secondary Emotions**: Help users access fear/longing beneath anger/withdrawal
- **Attachment Injuries**: Recognize moments of betrayal/abandonment that need repair

**Effectiveness**: ~70% recovery rate; gains maintained 2+ years post-treatment

**App Integration Points**:
- Cycle-identification tools ("What's your dance?")
- Emotion processing prompts that distinguish anger from fear
- Repair conversation templates
- Partner accessibility check-ins

---

#### **GOTTMAN METHOD COUPLES THERAPY**
*Drs. John & Julie Gottman — "The Sound Relationship House"*

**Core Theory**:
- Friendship is the foundation of lasting love
- Conflict management matters more than conflict resolution (69% of conflicts are perpetual)
- Positive-to-negative ratio must be 5:1 or higher during conflict
- The "Four Horsemen" predict divorce: Criticism, Contempt, Defensiveness, Stonewalling

**Sound Relationship House Framework**:
1. Build Love Maps (know partner's inner world)
2. Share Fondness and Admiration
3. Turn Toward Instead of Away
4. The Positive Perspective
5. Manage Conflict (not resolve—manage)
6. Make Life Dreams Come True
7. Create Shared Meaning

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Four Horsemen & Antidotes**: Criticism→Gentle Start-Up; Contempt→Build Culture of Appreciation; Defensiveness→Take Responsibility; Stonewalling→Physiological Self-Soothing
- **Bids for Connection**: Track turning toward vs. away
- **Repair Attempts**: Recognize and respond to de-escalation bids
- **Flooding**: Recognize when physiological arousal exceeds capacity (HR>100)
- **Harsh vs. Soft Start-Up**: How conversations begin predicts how they end (96% accuracy)

**Effectiveness**: 18-month follow-up shows sustained gains; reduces divorce predictors

**App Integration Points**:
- Daily Love Map questions
- Appreciation journal/prompts
- Four Horsemen recognition training
- Repair attempt scripts
- Flooding detection (with wearable integration potential)
- Soft start-up templates for difficult conversations

---

#### **INTEGRATIVE BEHAVIORAL COUPLE THERAPY (IBCT)**
*Jacobson & Christensen*

**Core Theory**:
- Relationship problems stem from Differences, Emotional Sensitivities, External Circumstances, and Patterns of interaction (DEEP)
- Balance acceptance AND change strategies
- "Contingency-shaped" change (in-the-moment experience) vs. "rule-governed" change (teaching skills)
- Emotional acceptance of partner's differences is often more powerful than trying to change them

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Unified Detachment**: Step back and view the pattern as "it" rather than "you" vs. "me"
- **Soft Disclosure**: Express hurt underneath the anger
- **Tolerance Building**: Strategies to accept inevitable irritations
- **Difference → Asset**: Reframing incompatibilities as complementary

**Effectiveness**: 5-year follow-up data; ~69% clinically significant improvement; effectiveness validated in VA system dissemination

**App Integration Points**:
- DEEP analysis framework for self-understanding
- Pattern identification ("This is our X pattern showing up")
- Acceptance-focused reflections
- Reframing exercises for perpetual problems

### Tier 2: Strong Theoretical Foundation, Growing Evidence

#### **ACCEPTANCE AND COMMITMENT THERAPY (ACT) FOR COUPLES**
*Steven Hayes et al.*

**Core Theory**:
- Psychological flexibility is the key to wellbeing
- Six core processes: Defusion, Acceptance, Present Moment, Self-as-Context, Values, Committed Action
- Problems arise from fusion with thoughts and experiential avoidance
- Values-driven living creates meaning even amid difficulty

**2024 Meta-Analysis Finding**: ACT is effective for marital intimacy, communication, psychological well-being; comparable to IBCT and EFCT for certain outcomes

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Values Clarification**: What kind of partner do you want to be?
- **Defusion**: Creating distance from unhelpful relationship narratives
- **Acceptance**: Making room for difficult emotions without acting reactively
- **Committed Action**: Small steps aligned with relationship values
- **Willingness**: Choosing discomfort in service of what matters

**App Integration Points**:
- Relationship values exercises
- "Unhook" from reactive thoughts prompts
- Willingness practices for difficult conversations
- Values-guided behavior tracking

---

#### **PSYCHOBIOLOGICAL APPROACH TO COUPLE THERAPY (PACT)**
*Dr. Stan Tatkin*

**Core Theory**:
- Integration of attachment theory, developmental neuroscience, and arousal regulation
- Partners can become "expert" on each other's nervous systems
- "Secure functioning" principles: fairness, justice, sensitivity, collaboration
- Focus on real-time, moment-to-moment nonverbal communication

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Couple Bubble**: Creating a protective two-person system
- **Arousal Regulation**: Understanding and managing each other's activation
- **Procedural Memory**: Relationship patterns are implicit/automatic
- **Attachment Adaptations**: How early experiences wire current responses
- **Secure Functioning Principles**: Mutual protection, mutual benefit

**App Integration Points**:
- Understanding partner's triggers and soothing strategies
- Pre-conflict check-ins (nervous system state)
- "Expert on my partner" knowledge building
- Co-regulation exercises

---

#### **INTERNAL FAMILY SYSTEMS (IFS) FOR COUPLES**
*Richard Schwartz; Couples application: Toni Herbine-Blank's IFIO*

**Core Theory**:
- The mind contains multiple "parts" with different perspectives and functions
- Managers, Firefighters, and Exiles organize around protection
- The "Self" is inherently compassionate, curious, calm, confident
- Relational healing happens when partners can meet each other from Self rather than reactive parts

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Parts Recognition**: "A part of me feels..." language
- **U-Turn**: Turning attention inward to own reactivity before blaming partner
- **Self-Leadership**: Responding from centered Self vs. triggered part
- **Unburdening**: Releasing limiting beliefs carried from past

**Evidence Note**: Growing research base; 2025 scoping review identifies as "promising" for depression, PTSD, self-compassion

**App Integration Points**:
- Parts identification journal
- U-Turn prompts during conflict
- Self-leadership check-ins
- "Which part of me is activated right now?"

### Tier 3: Neuroscience Foundation (Informing Intervention Design)

#### **POLYVAGAL THEORY**
*Dr. Stephen Porges*

**Core Theory**:
- The autonomic nervous system has three states: Ventral Vagal (social engagement), Sympathetic (fight/flight), Dorsal Vagal (freeze/shutdown)
- "Neuroception" = unconscious detection of safety vs. threat
- Co-regulation = nervous systems influence each other
- Healing requires creating conditions of felt safety

**Key Constructs for App**:
- **Window of Tolerance**: Optimal arousal zone for productive engagement
- **Cues of Safety**: Voice tone, facial expression, body language that signal "no threat"
- **Co-Regulation**: Using partner's presence to regulate own state
- **Physiological State → Behavior**: Understanding that dysregulation drives reactivity

**App Integration Points**:
- State awareness tracking (ventral/sympathetic/dorsal)
- Co-regulation exercises (synchronized breathing, eye contact)
- Pre-conversation nervous system check
- Recovery strategies when flooded/shut down

## PART 3: ALGORITHM FRAMEWORK

### The "Three Black Boxes" — Onboarding Framework

Before diving into comprehensive assessment, help users identify where they have the *least clarity*:

> **"Where is your relationship's blind spot?"**
> 
> 1. **Attachment** — Why do I react the way I do when I feel disconnected?
> 2. **Conflict Patterns** — How do we fight, and what makes it escalate or repair?
> 3. **Personality** — How does our hard-wiring interact (and clash)?

This personalizes the assessment journey—rather than overwhelming with everything at once, prioritize based on the couple's felt sense of where they're stuck.

---

### Micro-Assessment Architecture (UX Critical)

Traditional clinical assessments kill engagement (the full Gottman Checkup is 337 questions). Design for mobile reality:

| Cadence | Format | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Onboarding** | Comprehensive (ECR-R, Big Five, baseline) | Full picture |
| **Weekly** | CSI-4 (4 items, 60 seconds) | Track satisfaction trend |
| **Daily** | 1-3 rotating micro-pulses | Real-time awareness without fatigue |
| **Event-triggered** | Deeper domain dive | After conflict, after connection, after repair attempt |

**Micro-pulse examples:**
- "How connected did you feel to your partner today?" (1-10)
- "Did you make a bid for connection today? Did they respond?"
- "What emotion showed up most in your relationship today?"

### Journey Architecture: Getting from A → B

**Phase 1: DISCOVERY (Weeks 1-2)**
- Complete comprehensive assessments
- Generate personalized relationship map
- Identify 2-3 priority growth areas
- Establish baseline metrics
- Create shared language ("This is our pursue-withdraw dance")

**Phase 2: AWARENESS (Weeks 3-6)**
- Pattern recognition training
- Attachment style education (what do I do when I feel...)
- Nervous system literacy (what state am I in?)
- Track real-time moments of connection and disconnection

**Phase 3: SKILL BUILDING (Weeks 7-12)**
- Targeted interventions for priority areas
- Practice exercises (with guided structure)
- Experiment with new responses
- Build tolerance for discomfort of doing things differently

**Phase 4: INTEGRATION (Ongoing)**
- New patterns become automatic
- Continued practice + check-ins
- Addressing deeper material as capacity increases
- Creating rituals that sustain connection

### Intervention Selection Logic

#### **The 5:1 Ratio — Core Tracking Metric**

Gottman's most robust finding: stable, happy couples maintain at least **5 positive interactions for every 1 negative interaction** during conflict. This is measurable and actionable:

```
TRACK: positive_interactions / negative_interactions

IF ratio >= 5.0 during conflict
   → Relationship in healthy zone
   → Reinforce what's working

IF ratio 3.0 - 5.0
   → Yellow zone
   → Suggest "appreciation boost" interventions
   → Increase positive bid prompts

IF ratio < 3.0
   → Red zone
   → Flag declining ratio to both partners
   → Prioritize repair conversation tools
   
IF ratio < 0.8 (more negative than positive)
   → High alert
   → Gentle prompt toward professional support
   → Increase check-in frequency
```

---

#### **Pattern-Based Intervention Selection**

```
IF Attachment Anxiety High (either partner):
   → Emphasize EFT accessibility exercises
   → Include reassurance practices
   → Watch for pursue pattern escalation

IF Attachment Avoidance High (either partner):
   → Emphasize gradual intimacy-building
   → Include nervous system regulation first
   → Don't push too fast into vulnerable sharing

IF Four Horsemen Present:
   → Prioritize Gottman antidotes
   → Contempt detected → URGENT culture of appreciation work
   → Stonewalling detected → Teach physiological self-soothing

IF Perpetual Problem Detected:
   → Apply IBCT acceptance strategies
   → Reframe through ACT values lens
   → Help find meaning within the difference

IF Trauma History Indicated:
   → Move slower
   → Prioritize nervous system regulation
   → Suggest IFS parts work
   → Consider referral for professional support

IF Low Intimacy (any domain):
   → Target specific intimacy domain exercises
   → Gradual intimacy building activities
   → Check for underlying avoidance or resentment blocking intimacy

IF High Neuroticism (either partner):
   → Prioritize nervous system regulation tools
   → More frequent grounding/co-regulation prompts
   → Frame interventions around emotional intensity management
   → Pair with partner's Agreeableness score for conflict predictions

IF Personality Mismatch Detected (e.g., High Introversion + High Extraversion):
   → Normalize difference as complementary, not defective
   → Build understanding of different "recharge" needs
   → Create rituals that honor both styles
```

### "Help Me" Conversation Framework

When a user reports needing help in the moment:

```
STEP 1: ASSESS STATE
"Before we dive in, let's check: Where are you right now?"
   → Calm enough to think clearly (ventral vagal)
   → Activated/upset (sympathetic)
   → Shut down/numb (dorsal vagal)

STEP 2: REGULATE FIRST (if needed)
"Let's take a moment to get grounded before problem-solving"
   → Breathing exercise
   → Physical grounding
   → Validation of feelings

STEP 3: UNDERSTAND THE SITUATION
"What happened?" → Focus on observable events
"What came up for you?" → Identify emotions and thoughts
"What do you need right now?" → Clarify immediate need vs. long-term

STEP 4: REFLECT THE PATTERN
"This sounds like it might be connected to [X pattern we've identified]"
→ Normalize ("This is your protect-and-defend part showing up")
→ Connect to attachment ("When you feel X, you tend to Y")

STEP 5: OFFER OPTIONS
"A few things that might help right now:"
   → Self-soothing (if flooded)
   → Repair script (if ready to reconnect)
   → Journaling prompt (if processing needed)
   → Request for partner response (if both engaged)

STEP 6: CLOSE THE LOOP
"How did that land?"
"Is there anything else you need right now?"
"Would you like to share this with [partner] or keep it private?"
```

## PART 4: ADDITIONAL EXPERT RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. SAFETY FIRST
- **Always screen for intimate partner violence** before launching into relationship work
- Include clear pathways to professional help when needed
- Build in crisis resources and escalation protocols
- Never recommend "working it out" if there are safety concerns

### 2. INDIVIDUAL + RELATIONAL
- Some work must be done individually (understanding own attachment, regulating own nervous system)
- Some work must be done together (repair, co-regulation, shared meaning)
- App should support both modes with clear handoff points

### 3. PACING IS EVERYTHING
- Trauma-informed design means going slow enough for the nervous system
- "Window of tolerance" concept should guide content delivery
- Better to do less deeply than more superficially

### 4. GAMIFICATION WITH CAUTION
- Relationship work is not a game to "win"
- Gamification elements should reinforce consistent practice, not competition
- Avoid scoring that could create shame or defensiveness
- Celebrate effort and process, not just outcomes

### 5. PROFESSIONAL INTEGRATION
- App should complement, not replace, therapy
- Build "therapist-ready" reports that couples can bring to sessions
- Include therapist finder with evidence-based modality filters
- Consider professional review/consultation tier

### 6. SPIRITUAL/CONTEMPLATIVE DIMENSION
- Many couples find meaning through shared spiritual practice
- Mindfulness exercises support regulation and presence
- Gratitude practices have strong evidence for relationship satisfaction
- Consider optional spiritual/contemplative track

### 7. CONTENT PRINCIPLES
- Use "I" language modeling throughout
- Normalize struggle ("All couples fight; healthy couples repair")
- Validate multiple perspectives simultaneously
- Never pathologize normal relationship challenges
- Model secure functioning in app voice itself
